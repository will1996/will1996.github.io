I want to use Generative AI, and Deep Learning to make better games, faster.

I set out to be a physics researcher, I started writing machine learning algorithms in a condensed matter physics lab, but it was my love of video games that inspired me to get a computer science degree. During my time in physics I was always trying to make physics engines, or little asteroids clones. Never in my wildest dreams did I ever think I would end up as Lead AI Programmer at Oxide Games.

I got a minor in computer science along with my first bachelors, that and my extensive math background got me a job writing Digital Signal Processing (DSP) algorithms. It was a great first job which got me started in paralell C++, SIMD, as well as the iterative process of making useful GUI and python interfaces for other teams. I knew I wanted to make games, though. So I started writing an engine myself, I made a lot of progress, but quickly realized that I needed a lot more education if I was going to know everything I needed to. I reenrolled in my Bachelors while working full time, then on to my masters.

Between my job, course work on classical AI and deep learning, and my game engine projects I learned fast, after two years I applied to oxide games, passed all the interviews, and got the job!

Working at Oxide has been tough, the deadlines are fast, and the problems are really hard, but it's better than I could have imagined. I earned the trust of the team fast, that trust has built a truly collaborative environment where amazing things happen every day. I love Oxide and I couldn't be more proud of how our game is shaping up!

Outside work I am seeking to make games differently, my experience at Oxide has shown me the challenges of making games on strict timelines; my research is focused on adressing those challenges with the latest in generative AI.
